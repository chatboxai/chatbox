# DEMO-PENCIL-001 Design Brief

## Metadata

- Story ID: DEMO-PENCIL-001
- Story Title: About Page Support Hub Refresh
- Author: Codex
- Date: 2026-04-01

## Audience / Entry Context

- Primary audience: Existing Chatbox users opening the About page for trust,
  support, or community links.
- What brings them to this surface now: They need help, want to verify the app
  version, or want a clear next route for feedback or community contact.
- What they likely know before landing: They already know Chatbox and do not
  need a marketing introduction.
- What they need to decide or do next: Pick the right support, feedback,
  changelog, legal, or community destination quickly.

## Desired Feeling

- Primary feeling to create: Guided and trustworthy.
- Secondary feelings to support: Calm, service-oriented, and clearly organized.
- Feelings to avoid: Generic dashboard, flashy marketing splash, or flat
  settings dump.
- Why this emotional posture fits the story: The page should feel like a real
  support hub, not a loose list of links.

## Design Language Translation

- Cue 1: Concierge desk rather than control panel.
- Cue 2: Editorial grouping with clear route labels.
- Cue 3: Calm surfaces with visible emphasis on the most useful next actions.
- Cue 4: Supportive hierarchy that answers "where should I go next?"
- Cue 5: Service-signage clarity over decorative flourish.
- Optional cue 6: Human help-center tone rather than product-console tone.
- Optional cue 7: Intentional callouts for trust and update status.
- Anti-cues to avoid: Dense dashboard widgets, generic promo hero, loud
  marketing gradients, or anonymous placeholder labels.

## System Direction

- Neutral role: Existing Chatbox surfaces remain the canvas; do not introduce a
  new token family for this demo.
- Primary role: High-contrast text and headings carry the route hierarchy and
  version or legal trust signals.
- Secondary role: Supporting labels and helper text clarify categories without
  competing with primary actions.
- Accent role: Existing Chatbox accent treatments should pull attention to the
  most useful next actions and notices only.
- Typography posture: Direct, legible, and help-center oriented rather than
  promotional.
- Component or surface character: Support-hub blocks, route tiles, and notices
  should feel intentional and serviceable.

## Layout Metaphor

- Physical-object or editorial analogy: Support concierge wall with clearly
  grouped destinations.
- Why this metaphor fits: Users need fast route selection with enough trust and
  context to pick the right destination confidently.
- Variation axis 1: Hero-guided versus evenly distributed route hierarchy.
- Variation axis 2: Editorial split-pane versus tidy support-hub grouping.
- Variation axis 3: Higher narrative tone versus denser utility presentation.

## Copy Direction

- Copy change status: small edit
- Voice and tone: Calm, direct, and service-oriented.
- Naming posture: Concrete route names over abstract slogans.
- CTA posture: Actionable and specific, with no hype language.

## Constraints / No-Go Decisions

- Scope constraints: About page only.
- Content constraints: Preserve the existing destinations and legal links.
- Accessibility constraints: Maintain clear grouping, readable hierarchy, and
  responsive layouts on small and large screens.
- Implementation constraints: Stay grounded in the current Chatbox token system
  and existing Mantine route patterns.
- Explicit no-go decisions: No new backend behavior, no new APIs, no major
  translation expansion, and no marketing-landing-page treatment.
